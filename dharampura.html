Haveli Dharampura

Location: 2293, Gali Guliyan, Dharampur, Chandni Chowk, Old Delhi Time Period: Around 1887, Late Mughal Haveli Significance: Architecture

Not much is known about this Haveli, built in the late 1800s. Spread over 550 sq. metres, it is a beautiful Haveli built around a central courtyard. There are a lot of intricate decorations, like woodwork on the roof, sculptures, balconies, stone windows known as Jakhoras, floral doorframes and arches. The building itself is constructed of lakhori bricks (burnt clay) and lime mortar, and the flooring is made of lime concrete and wood.

Until recently, the Haveli was in shambles. There were numerous additions to the property, it was encroached on by shops and squatters in rooms and the whole structure was breaking down. A restoration was completed from 2011 to 2016, resulting in possibly the only Mughal haveli in good condition today. Considerations: It is a hotel, and apparently cars aren’t allowed on the road that it’s situated on.
