ABOUT THE PROJECT

The Delhi Monument Project is a website dedicated to telling the history of the city of Delhi, and in extension India as a whole, by showcasing many monuments – many of which are not generally known. Through them, I wish to tell the history of the different empires which have ruled India. For easy accessibility, the monuments have been divided into four eras: Pre-Sultanate, Sultanate, Mughal, and British.

About Me: My name is Vedant Venkatesh and I am a student at the Shri Ram School - Aravali in Gurgaon, graduating in 2025. I am interested in history and philosophy, and was inspired by William Dalrymple’s “City of Djinns” to create this website and better understand the history of the city in which I live.

A Note on the Sources: I have personally travelled to these monuments except where I do not have permission to enter. The information is credited to sources on the internet, research papers, the Archaeological Survey of India (which makes informative plaques at monuments), and the guides and security guards at the monuments (fact-checked, of course). The pictures have been taken by me, except where I cannot due to a lack of permission, and are taken with a Nikon D40X camera. The panoramas have been created through Adobe Photoshop. The 360˚ pictures at the bottom of the pages have been embedded from Google Street View. The fonts used are Adobe Garamond Pro and Neutraface.
