SATPULA

Satpula was a historic Tughlaq-era dam. It was built as a part of the 4th wall of Jahanpanah (which connected Mehrauli and Siri). It functioned as a dam, which used the water resources (Barapulla Nallah) that were feeding the River Yamuna. It was a great solution to the irrigation problems that plagued the city at that time (there were recurring droughts and famines).

The dam itself had 11 arched bays and 7 sluice gates, covering a total length of almost 60 metres. On either side of the dam were 2 octagonal towers. The name ‘satpula’ means seven bridges
