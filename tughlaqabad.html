Tughlaqabad Fort

Time Period: Constructed between 1320 and 1324 Location: Near the ‘Govind Puri’ metro station Significance: A fort which used to surround Tughlaqabad (3rd City of Delhi)

In 1320, the founder of the Tughlaq Dynasty (of the Delhi Sultanate), Ghiyas-ud-din-Tughlaq, commissioned an architect (probably a nobleman named Ahmad B. Ayaz) to build a fort to protect his new imperial capital (Tughlaqabad). Although this city was never occupied and instead abandoned by the next ruler, the grand fort was still built.

The fort was very strong, because it was made of rubble and hammer dressed quartzite stone set in lime mortar, and the fort walls were built in three tiers with the lower two with a ledge and a gallery. Finally, all three layers were protected by battlements.
